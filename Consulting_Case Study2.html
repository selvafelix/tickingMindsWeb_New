Case Study: Testing Tool Optimization for a leading loyalty management solutions provider


Client Background

    Client is leading product company that develops and operates professional customer loyalty programmes based on tailor-made IT solutions and process management systems. Their loyalty solutions have always set highest standards in the respective industries.


Business Context

    Client had big plans for the launch of their flagship product which was undergoing re-engineering from a technology stack perspective and had strategies in place for wide variety of testing, but wanted to be sure about the tool selection for automating different types of prime tests - automation, performance, test data- as well as make appropriate recommendation on toolkit factoring for technology support and future product road maps. The client had very short time to decide on tools and were looking for quick and concrete solution for them.


How did we help the client?

1.  Understood the overall testing needs of the client, identified types of testing to be carried out and what could be automated to the maximum extent.

2.  Next, we understood the logical architecture, the associated technology stack used along the different layers and came up with an overall automation tool kit to cover all layers as well as tests.

3.  Deep dived into tools analysis, catering to automation of different types of tests at the same time keep the technology stack and budget availability of the client.

4.  Analyzed existing tools used by the customer to explore potential for reuse of tools for some of the tests and made quick study of their existing tool spend.

5.  Recommended an apt mix of tools highlighting two options- open source and commercial tools- laid out SWOT analysis of them for easy decision making to choose the tools to be part of the overall automation tool kit.

6.  Laid out an implementation road map for automation tool kit covering performance, test data and functional tests.


Benefits Delivered

1.  Saved considerable OPEX for the customer by advising them to stop renewing some of the unwarranted commercial tool licenses and to go with wider set of open source tools.

2.  Indigenous automation tool kit that provided maximum automation coverage across different types of tests.

2.  Strong quality improvement early through end to end automation tool kit.

2.  Improved reusability of scripts - reuse of functional test scripts for performance tests- that provided considerable effort savings.
